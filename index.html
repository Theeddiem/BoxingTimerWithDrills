Shadow Boxing Workout

  • Use Your Legs: Generate power from your legs by pushing off the back foot when throwing punches. The power starts from the ground and travels through your body to the point of contact.
  • Rotate Your Hips and Shoulders: Engage your hips and shoulders when throwing punches. Rotate your body to maximize the power and reach of your punches.
  • Hand Position: Keep your hands up near your face when not punching, and bring them back quickly after each punch. This helps with both defense and quick counterattacks.
  • Chin Tucked Behind Your Lead Shoulder: Your chin should be tucked behind your lead shoulder, This position helps protect your chin from direct hits.
  • Chin Down: Ensure that your chin is slightly tucked down. This minimizes the target area for your opponent and adds an extra layer of defense.
  • Movement After Throwing a Punch: Incorporate head movement, angle changes, and distance control after delivering a punch to avoid counters and create better offensive opportunities.
  • Head Movement: Immediately after throwing a punch, incorporate head movement. This can include slipping to the side, bobbing and weaving, or rolling your head to avoid your opponent's counter punches.
  • Change Angles: Pivot or step to the side after throwing a punch to change angles. This makes it more difficult for your opponent to track your movements and counter effectively.
  • Step Back or Lateral Movement: Take a step back or move laterally to create distance after delivering a punch. This helps you evade potential counters and reassess the situation.
  • Circle Away: Move in a circular pattern away from your opponent after throwing a punch. This can disrupt their line of sight and set you up for further offensive or defensive maneuvers.
